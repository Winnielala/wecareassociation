    First Covid-19 cases in Malaysia

    25th January 2020

    On the 25th of January 2020, the first case of COVID-19 was detected in Malaysia and traced back to 3 Chinese nationals who previously had close contact with an infected person in Singapore. The first Malaysian was confirmed with COVID-19 on the 4th of February 2020, he is 41-year-old man and had recently returned from Singapore when he started to develop a fever and a cough.

    New Daily Record with 13,215 cases

    15th of July 2021

    Malaysia has recorded 13,215 Covid-19 cases on Thursday (July 15), a new daily record.The highest figure previously was 11,618 cases on July 14.Selangor recorded an all-time high of 6,120 new cases, and Kuala Lumpur recorded 1,499 new infections.The total number of cases in the country has now reached 880,782.

    Malaysia's daily vaccination rate of Covid-19 has break the record

    8th of July 2021

    Yesterday (July 8th), Malaysia was vaccinated with 376,909 doses of Covid-19 vaccines, which was the fourth consecutive day that Robertson was vaccinated with more than 300,000 shots. This is the highest number of shots recorded every day.

    Completed List of MCO SOP

    • All economic and social activities are not allowed to operate except for 17 essential services such as F&B, health and medical sectors, vets and animal clinics, water, energy, security, communications, post and courier services, banking, insurance, agriculture, fishing, and more.

    • Allow selected departments in the manufacturing industry to operate with 60% of employees' capacity.

    • Except for tenants who belong to basic services category, such as supermarkets, restaurants, convenience stores, optical shops, pharmacies, etc., shopping malls are not allowed to operate.

    • All F&B outlets are allowed to operate for delivery and takeaway services only (strictly no dine-ins and park & dine allowed).

    • Most services will only be allowed to operate from 8AM - 8PM daily.

    • There will be no curfew, and Malaysians are urged to stay at home after 8 pm.

    • Only 2 people are allowed to go to buy daily necessities, such as food, groceries and medicine in each family.

    • Only 2 passengers (including the driver) are allowed per car in taxis and e-hailing vehicles.

    • All schools and education institutions will be closed throughout the MCO. Childcare centres (taska) and private (or international) kindergartens are allowed only to operate for children in the situation whereby both parents work in essential services.

    • Face-to-face meetings, seminars, trainings, exhibitions, talks, and conferences cannot be held.

    • Social events such as weddings, receptions, birthday celebrations, reunions, retreats, akad nikah, and more are strictly prohibited.

    • Tourism activities and attractions such as aquariums, indoor playgrounds, zoos, farms, nature parks, museums, art galleries, karaoke centres, and more are not allowed to operate.

    • Citizens who need to cross district lines to head for their vaccination appointments do not require a permission letter from the police and can simply flash their proof of appointment on the MySejahtera app, website, or SMS if stopped by authorities.
